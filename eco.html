The Hidden Environmental Tax

Data centers aren't carbon neutral. Bandwidth isn't free.

When an AI agent from OpenAI, Anthropic, or an e-commerce crawler visits your site, it downloads your entire HTML structure. That includes navigation bars, dense CSS classes, redundant inline styles, and JavaScript that the agent can't even execute.

A standard 3,000-word article arrives as roughly 16,000 tokens of HTML soup. Transferring, parsing, and storing that extraneous data consumes vast amounts of energy at both your origin server and the AI provider's data centers.

AXO strips the bloat, delivering raw, structured Markdown to agents. It drops a 16,000 token payload down to 3,000 tokens. That's an 80% drop in energy consumption per request.
